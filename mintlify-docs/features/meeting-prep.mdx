The prep page helps users walk into a meeting with the right context already assembled.

![Deal brief before the call](/images/deal-brief-before-call.png)

## What users can do

- edit the meeting title if needed
- build or refine the agenda
- add goals and desired outcomes
- add contextual notes
- review attendee and company brief information
- attach sources that Shadow can use during the meeting

## What the prep surface includes

- an **Agenda** workflow with editable agenda items
- a **Briefing** view for researched meeting context
- goal, context, and desired outcomes fields
- time allocation and agenda item typing
- attached sources that can later be used during the meeting

## Agenda behavior

If an agenda does not already exist, Shadow can generate one automatically from the meeting context. Users can then:

- rename agenda items
- adjust time allocations
- change item types such as discuss, decide, update, or present
- add or remove talking points
- refine the goal and desired outcomes

The prep page is meant to be editable, not just read-only.

## Typical prep workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the meeting from the dashboard">
    Start from the upcoming meeting list and open the prep view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review or generate the agenda">
    Add, edit, or refine agenda items, confirm timing, and make the meeting structure explicit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the goal and outcomes">
    Capture what success should look like before the meeting starts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review brief information">
    Check the meeting room, external attendees, and company context.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attach sources">
    Add notes, docs, links, or uploaded files that Shadow may need during the call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the meeting from prep">
    Move into the live meeting workflow once the meeting is ready to begin.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## When to use it

- Before customer calls
- Before discovery meetings
- Before deal reviews
- Before internal meetings where decisions or follow-through matter

## Best practices

- Set the meeting goal before generating or refining the agenda.
- Use the Briefing view when the user needs account or attendee context before the call.
- Attach the few sources that matter most instead of flooding the meeting with low-signal material.
