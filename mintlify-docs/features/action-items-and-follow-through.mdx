---
title: "Action Items and Follow-Through"
description: "Review, approve, clarify, and route the next steps that come out of meetings."
---

Shadow is most valuable when the meeting does not stop at a transcript or recap.

## What Shadow helps with

- extract likely next steps
- categorize or group follow-through
- stage actions for user approval
- prepare work in external tools

## What users review here

- who owns the next step
- where the follow-through should go
- whether the wording is clear enough to execute
- whether the proposed destination is correct

In the product, these can appear as structured action threads rather than as a loose checklist.

## Operating model

- Shadow proposes
- the user reviews
- the user approves or clarifies
- only then should external work be created or updated

## Examples of follow-through

- draft an Outlook follow-up email
- create a calendar follow-up
- post a Slack summary
- create or update Jira or Linear work
- push CRM updates to Salesforce

## Why the approval step matters

This is especially important for:

- customer-facing emails
- CRM updates
- ticket creation
- routing work to external tools the user cares about operationally

<Tip>
  Approval-based actions are the safer rollout model for customer-facing and revenue workflows.
</Tip>
