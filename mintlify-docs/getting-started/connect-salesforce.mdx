Salesforce is not a general-purpose setup step for every Shadow user.

In the current product, Salesforce connection management is intended for **enterprise admins**. It is used after meetings so Shadow can propose CRM updates and let the user approve what should be written back.

## What Salesforce is used for

- Propose new leads
- Propose new contacts
- Propose new opportunities
- Surface CRM-ready updates after the meeting

## Connect Salesforce

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm you are the enterprise admin">
    Salesforce connection management is meant for the enterprise admin, not for every member in the org.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Integrations">
    Open the Integrations page in Shadow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect Salesforce">
    Click connect on the Salesforce card and finish the OAuth flow in your browser.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Return to Shadow">
    When the browser flow completes, return to the desktop app and confirm Salesforce shows as connected.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What happens after a meeting

If Salesforce is connected, Shadow can analyze meeting context and prepare proposals on the review page. The user can then push approved proposals to Salesforce.

<Note>
  The review flow is approval-based. Users stay in control of what gets written back into Salesforce.
</Note>

<Warning>
  If a user is not an enterprise admin, they should not be instructed to connect Salesforce as part of their personal onboarding flow.
</Warning>
