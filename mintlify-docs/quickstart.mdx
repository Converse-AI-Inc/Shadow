This quickstart gets a new user from zero to a complete first workflow in Shadow:

1. Install the Mac app
2. Grant permissions
3. Sign in and connect the right calendar path
4. Join a meeting
5. Review the meeting output and follow through

## Prerequisites

- A Mac that can run the Shadow desktop app
- An active Shadow account
- Access to the Google or Microsoft account the user wants to use with Shadow
- A calendar-backed meeting with a valid join link

## Get started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install Shadow on your Mac">
    Download the latest Mac app from [GitHub Releases](https://github.com/Converse-AI-Inc/Shadow/releases/latest/download/Shadow.dmg), open `Shadow.dmg`, move Shadow into your Applications folder, and then launch the app.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Grant the required permissions">
    Approve Accessibility, Microphone, and System Audio permissions so Shadow can detect meetings and capture audio during supported calls.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in with the right provider">
    Use Google for the standard individual path, or Microsoft for the Outlook and Teams path. Both sign-in flows request calendar access during login.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm upcoming meetings appear">
    Open the dashboard and confirm that your upcoming meetings show up in **Next Up**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open an upcoming meeting">
    Use the dashboard to open the meeting prep view, review the context, and then join the call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use Shadow during the meeting">
    Keep the live meeting view or overlay open to see transcript, live cards, approvals, and meeting guidance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and follow through">
    After the meeting, open the review page to confirm the summary and action items. Enterprise admins can also review Salesforce proposals if Salesforce is connected.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What success looks like

At the end of this quickstart, the user should be able to:

- see upcoming meetings on the dashboard
- open a meeting prep page before the call
- use Shadow during a supported meeting
- review summary and action items after the call
