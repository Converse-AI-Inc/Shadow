The dashboard is the main operating surface for Shadow.

## What users do here

- see upcoming meetings
- open meeting prep
- join a meeting workflow
- launch the assistant manually
- open recent meeting reviews

## Key sections

- `Next Up`: the most relevant imminent calendar meeting
- `Upcoming meetings`: scheduled calls from the user's connected calendar
- `Past meetings`: previously recorded or processed meetings
- `Launch Assistant`: manual start for ad hoc or unsupported flows

## What users can do from the dashboard

### Open prep before the meeting

Users can open an upcoming meeting and go to the prep workflow before the call starts.

### Join a scheduled meeting

From **Next Up** or the upcoming meetings list, users can open the meeting URL and enter the live workflow.

When the user joins during the active meeting window, Shadow can start the recording flow immediately. Outside that window, Shadow still opens the meeting context, but avoids starting the recording too early.

### Launch Shadow without a calendar event

Users can click **Launch Assistant** to start a manual recording workflow for ad hoc meetings or for meetings that were not entered through the calendar path.

### Return to past meetings

Users can open previous meetings from the dashboard to review summaries, transcript, action items, and follow-through.

## Calendar-driven behavior

- upcoming meetings are sourced from the user's connected calendar
- meetings with valid meeting links can appear in **Next Up**
- Shadow can schedule a pre-meeting notification before calendar-backed meetings

## Best practices

- Use the dashboard as the default starting point each day.
- Open the prep page before the meeting instead of joining cold.
- Use the dashboard for scheduled meetings and **Launch Assistant** for ad hoc ones.
- Use past meetings from the dashboard to revisit summaries and action items.
