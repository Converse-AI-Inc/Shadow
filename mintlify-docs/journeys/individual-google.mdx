This journey is for users on the free or pro plan who are setting up Shadow for themselves rather than through an enterprise rollout.

## Best-fit user

- Uses a Mac
- Wants to sign in with Google
- Wants calendar-backed meeting visibility
- Does not need enterprise user management
- Does not need enterprise Salesforce setup

## Plan differences that matter

- **Free** includes a monthly meeting-minute limit
- **Pro** removes the meeting-minute limit

The product flow is otherwise similar for an individual user.

## Individual journey

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install Shadow on your Mac">
    Download the latest Mac app from [GitHub Releases](https://github.com/Converse-AI-Inc/Shadow/releases/latest/download/Shadow.dmg), open `Shadow.dmg`, move Shadow to Applications, and launch it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in with Google">
    Choose **Continue with Google**. During sign-in, Shadow requests Google Calendar access so it can read upcoming meetings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Grant Mac permissions">
    Approve Accessibility, Microphone, and Screen or System Audio permissions so Shadow can detect and support live meetings on macOS.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm your meetings appear">
    Open the dashboard and check that upcoming calendar meetings appear in **Next Up**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run your first meeting">
    Open a meeting from the dashboard, use the prep view, then join and run Shadow during the meeting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the output">
    After the meeting, use the review page to confirm the summary, transcript, and action items.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What individual users generally do not need

- org invites
- user role management
- enterprise visibility settings
- admin-managed Salesforce connection

## What success looks like

- The user signs in with Google
- Calendar meetings appear in Shadow
- The user can launch a meeting successfully
- The meeting review page produces useful output

## Common failure points

- Google calendar consent is skipped or not completed
- macOS permissions are skipped
- The user reaches the free plan usage limit and cannot launch more meeting time that month
