The People section turns Shadow into a relationship view that builds itself from your meetings. Every distinct person you have met shows up automatically — no data entry, no contact import.

## What users do here

- see everyone they have met, deduped across email addresses
- find a person fast with search, sort, and External/Internal tabs
- open a profile to review meeting history, company, and open follow-ups
- pin the people they care about most
- correct a name, company, or title inline
- merge two entries when the same person used two emails

## How the list is built

People appears in the left sidebar of the desktop app. The list is populated from your calendar and meeting history:

- attendees on past and upcoming meetings are resolved into a single person per human
- the same person under multiple email addresses is folded together when Shadow is confident
- bots, room resources, no-reply senders, and your own account are filtered out
- enrichment (photo, title, company) is attached when available and persists between meetings
- the list refreshes in the background; you can also force a rebuild from the page

## Key fields on a person

- **Name, title, company** — from enrichment, with your inline edits winning over the computed value
- **Last met** — the most recent past meeting with that person
- **Next meeting** — the next scheduled meeting with that person, if any
- **Open loops** — outstanding action items tied to that person
- **Going cold** — flagged when an open loop has been silent for about two weeks
- **Pinned** — up to three people can be pinned to the top of the list

## Tabs, search, and sort

<Steps>
  <Step title="Switch between External and Internal">
    External shows people outside your company domain. Internal shows teammates.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search by name, email, or company">
    The search box filters the visible list as you type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sort the results">
    Choose **Recently met**, **Most met**, or **A–Z**. Pinned people stay at the top regardless of sort.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scroll for more">
    The list pages in additional results as you scroll.
  </Step>

## The profile drawer

Selecting a person opens a drawer with:

- header with photo, name, title, company, and primary email
- a meeting timeline with past meetings and the next scheduled meeting
- open action items tied to that person
- inline edit for name, company, and title
- a pin toggle

### Inline edits

Inline edits act as overrides. They win over enrichment values and survive future rebuilds. Clear an edit to fall back to the computed value.

### Pinning

You can pin up to three people. Pinned people sort to the top of the list, most recently pinned first. Unpin one to free up a slot.

### Merging two entries

If the same person appears twice — for example a personal address and a work address — open one entry and merge it with the other email. The merge is durable: it is reapplied every time the list rebuilds, so the two entries stay folded together going forward.

## Plan availability

- The populated People list, search, tabs, sort, pinning, and inline edits are available on all plans, including Free.
- Deeper meeting prep depth inside the profile drawer is part of paid plans. Upgrade prompts appear in the drawer where applicable.

## Best practices

- Pin the three people you most want to keep warm.
- Use the **Going cold** flag as a weekly nudge to follow up on stalled threads.
- Merge duplicate entries the first time you notice them — once merged, the link is permanent.
- Correct a wrong company or title once; the override persists across rebuilds.
- Use **Internal** to find a teammate quickly without scrolling past customers.
