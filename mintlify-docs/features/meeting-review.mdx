The review page is where the meeting becomes usable follow-through.

![Automatic recap and CRM updates](/images/automatic-recap-crm-updates.png)

## What appears on the review page

- summary
- action items
- action threads
- key decisions
- agenda notes
- transcript
- source usage
- meeting context
- Salesforce proposals when connected

## Important review behaviors

- if a transcript exists but a summary has not been generated yet, Shadow can generate it
- action threads may continue preparing in the background and then become reviewable
- transcript remains the source of truth when a summary or action needs verification
- source usage helps the user see what Shadow relied on during the meeting

## Org sharing and visibility

For org users, the review page can also reflect visibility state:

- external meetings may be visible to admins depending on org policy
- internal meetings can be shared intentionally
- members can use the internal meeting share toggle on their own meetings
- admins may see a visibility label when reviewing relevant meetings

## Typical review flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the meeting review page">
    Return from the live meeting flow or open the meeting later from the dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Validate the summary">
    Confirm that the recap matches the important outcomes of the meeting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check action items">
    Review owners, destinations, and clarity of each next step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review action threads and approvals">
    Confirm that follow-through is routed to the right destination before approving it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm decisions and notes">
    Look for missing or misframed decisions before using the output downstream.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the transcript when precision matters">
    Use the transcript as the source of truth for disputed or sensitive details.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What good review hygiene looks like

- summaries are checked before sharing externally
- action items are approved or clarified before execution
- CRM proposals are pushed only after the user confirms they are right
- internal meeting visibility is checked before sharing with an admin or team
