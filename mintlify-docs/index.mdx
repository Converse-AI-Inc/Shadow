Shadow is a Mac desktop meeting assistant for revenue teams. It helps users prepare for meetings, get live support during calls, and handle follow-through after the meeting ends.

Users can download the Mac app here: [Download Shadow for Mac](https://github.com/Converse-AI-Inc/Shadow/releases/latest/download/Shadow.dmg). For sales or rollout support, contact [hello@shadowlabs.ai](mailto:hello@shadowlabs.ai?subject=Shadow%20Documentation%20Question).

This documentation is organized around the onboarding and meeting workflows users actually run:

- install and onboard on Mac
- choose the right sign-in and setup path
- prep for a meeting
- use Shadow live during the call
- review outcomes and push follow-up work after the call

## What Shadow covers

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Choosing the right onboarding path">
    Shadow has separate onboarding paths for enterprise admins, enterprise members, and individual free or pro users. The correct journey depends on the user's plan and whether they use Google or Microsoft for sign-in.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Before the meeting">
    Use the dashboard to see upcoming calls, open the prep view, refine the agenda, add meeting context, and attach sources that Shadow can use during the conversation.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="During the meeting">
    Shadow runs on your Mac, detects supported meeting platforms, records with user-granted permissions, and surfaces live cards, transcript, and action suggestions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="After the meeting">
    Review the summary, action items, decisions, transcript, source usage, and Salesforce proposals. Then push the right updates into your system of record.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Start here

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install and launch Shadow">
    Start with the [Quickstart](/quickstart) and the [System Requirements](/getting-started/system-requirements).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the right journey">
    Use [User Journey Overview](/journeys/mac-outlook-teams-salesforce) to pick the right path for enterprise admin, enterprise member, or individual onboarding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Grant Mac permissions">
    Complete the required Mac permissions in [Permissions](/getting-started/permissions).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up only the tools that apply">
    Use [Connect Outlook](/getting-started/connect-outlook) for Microsoft-based setups, [Microsoft Teams](/integrations/microsoft-teams) for Teams-specific behavior, and [Connect Salesforce](/getting-started/connect-salesforce) only for enterprise admin CRM setup.

  <Step title="Run the full workflow">
    Use Shadow through prep, live meeting, and review.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  The most common rollout path is enterprise admin on Mac with Microsoft, Teams, and Salesforce, but the docs should always route users into the journey that matches their role and plan.
</Tip>
